Platform

Everything Wildland Fire Demands

Purpose-built for the chaos of initial attack and large wildland fire operations — not adapted from a law enforcement/police CAD system.

Dynamic Desktop

Open multiple incident windows simultaneously. Dockable panels for resources, timers, and checklists — each dispatcher gets the exact layout they need.

Live Map with Custom Layers

Add your own agency data — FuelMoisture, pretreatment areas, historical fire scars, PACE data — on top of real-time resource locations and perimeter feeds.

Resource Management

Track every engine, crew, dozer, aircraft, and overhead across your entire zone. Status, availability, location, and certification — all in one panel.

Smart Routing

Automatically calculate best routes for each resource type considering road access, fire perimeter, hazardous terrain, and current resource positions.

Incident Timers

Track critical operational windows — escape route timers, safety zone clearance times, briefing countdowns, and shift rotations — visible across all open incidents.

Multi-Agency Coordination

Share incidents and resources across dispatch zones. Federated access model allows BLM, USFS, state, and local agencies to work from the same operational picture.

Who It's For

Built for the Dispatch Center

Managing multiple concurrent fires across thousands of acres requires a workspace built to keep pace. WildCAD-E was designed by people who've sat in the dispatch center — every workflow, every shortcut, every decision support tool built for speed under pressure.

  • Multi-incident windows open simultaneously
  • One-click resource assignment and routing
  • Sparky AI surfacing critical information automatically
  • Customizable alerting thresholds and escalations
  • Full ICS forms integration and auto-population
  • Resource rostering and availability tracking
  • Multi-agency coordination across dispatch zones
  • Incident timeline summaries and audit trail

About

Built by the Best, for Those on the Line

WildCAD-E is a joint initiative between SAIC (Science Applications International Corporation) and Bighorn Information Systems (BIS), managed by the U.S. Department of the Interior and jointly funded with the U.S. Forest Service.

SAIC brings large-scale federal systems integration and engineering depth. BIS brings decades of deep expertise in wildland fire software. Together, they built the system that wildland fire dispatchers have needed for years — modern, cloud-native, and AI-forward.
